Mapping the Rono et al. (2024) brief to zdsim

Methods correspondence note · same article as Readme.md

Source document. Betsy Rono, Mambo Susan, Anne Njeri, Raphael Akangbe. Project 2-2A-7, April 2024. PDF: 2-2A-7_Rono_Zero-dose-vaccination.pdf (keep locally; optional docs/references/).

Abstract

Project 2-2A-7 outlines zero-dose vaccination concepts, Starsim-style modeling, and policy targets for Kenya—including the research question on tetanus cases averted under a 50% zero-dose reduction by 2025. This note records how that brief aligns with zdsim and run_simulation.py: which definitions and parameters match, which differ, and which claims require external survey or WHO sources rather than simulation output. It supports reviewers and reproducibility; it does not replace the original PDF.

Keywords: zero-dose children; DTP; pentavalent vaccine; Starsim; agent-based model; Kenya; Immunization Agenda 2030

1 Introduction

The brief situates zero-dose burden in KEPI/Gavi and WHO terms, poses the tetanus / 50% zero-dose-by-2025 research question, and proposes quantitative illustrations. What the repository reports for that question is stated first in Readme.md (Research question). zdsim implements administrative calibration and two delivery arms (reference vs intervention). This note does not reproduce the brief’s slides; it states what the code implements so readers can compare statements in the PDF to executable choices in the repository.

2 Terminology and analytic scope

BriefImplementation
Zero-dose (Gavi / KEPI): no first DTP-containing dose (DTP1), per NVIP MoH 2023 Modeled as no pentavalent dose in ZeroDoseVaccination; administrative DTP1 proxy from zerodose_data_formated.xlsx drives calibration.
IA2030 Described in Readme and SPA mission text; not a separate module.
Kenya, under-five focus Cohort is under-fives in the ABM; national/county geography is not modeled unless you add strata or spatial data.
DTP1 as operational ZD indicator (WHO) Mean DTP1 coverage proxy from monthly admin data → intervention_coverage; zero-dose proxy = 1 − coverage (see zerodose_data.py).

3 Correspondence of methods: brief vs this repository

BriefImplementation
Starsim Yes — ss.Sim in run_simulation.py (build_sim_from_bundle).
SIS structure in slides This repo uses Starsim Infection modules (S–I–R-style with disease-specific states), not a single SIS compartment for all pathogens.
Tetanus: γ = 3/12 yr, waning = 0.055 Yes in zdsim/diseases/tetanus.py (dur_inf mean 3/12 yr, waning=0.055).
Tetanus β = 1.3 Different: tetanus is modeled as non–person-to-person; bundle passes tetanus_beta into the module (default 0.02 in zerodose_calibration.py). The brief’s β may reflect a teaching formulation; do not equate without harmonizing model structure.
Intervention: vaccination prob = 0.25, efficacy = 0.9 Efficacy 0.9 matches intervention_efficacy=0.9 in the bundle. “Prob 0.25” maps to routine_prob × coverage in ZeroDoseVaccination; the intervention arm uses scale-up (routine_prob capped, higher coverage cap) — tune with --scale-routine-factor / --scale-coverage-cap.
50% reduction in ZD by 2025 Policy target, not hard-coded. Use --rono-2025-window (2024–2025 calendar window) for the brief’s horizon, and compare reference vs intervention relative reduction in modeled zero-dose share (relative_reduction_percent_model).
Research question: tetanus cases averted research_question_tetanus in zerodose_demo_summary.json — modeled new tetanus infections (reference − intervention). See Readme.md at the top.

4 Software configuration for the brief’s policy horizon

python run_simulation.py --rono-2025-window

This sets calendar 2024–2025 (overrides default --start / --stop). For longer policy projections, omit the flag and use e.g. --start 2025 --stop 2055.

5 External statistics cited in the brief

KDHS (~7% ZD), global WHO figures, county names, and similar quantities appear as citations in the PDF, not as outputs of this code. For publications, validate figures against KDHS, WUENIC, and WHO primary sources.

6 Discussion

The simulation yields modeled zero-dose shares and disease-attributable deaths in a cohort; it does not estimate national headcounts or subnational coverage unless extended. Parameters that match the brief (e.g. vaccine efficacy) should be read alongside structural differences (e.g. tetanus transmission assumptions). Use --rono-2025-window when comparing to the brief’s stated horizon; use longer windows for extended policy projections.
