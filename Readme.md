# Zero-dose children and vaccination impact (zdsim)

### Research question (Project 2-2A-7)

**How many tetanus cases will be averted if we reduce prevalence of zero-dose vaccination by 50% among under-fives by the year 2025?**

**Answer from this project:** After each run, `zerodose_demo_summary.json` includes **`research_question_tetanus`**. There, **`modeled_answer.tetanus_cases_averted_total`** is **modeled new tetanus infections** (Starsim tetanus module) in the **reference** arm minus the **intervention** arm over the projection window; **`tetanus_cases_averted_calendar_year_2025`** is the same contrast **restricted to calendar year 2025** when that year is in the run. **`modeled_zero_dose_relative_reduction_percent_end_window`** is the modeled zero-dose share reduction vs reference at the **end** of the window (the intervention is not hard-coded to 50%; tune delivery with `--scale-routine-factor` / `--scale-coverage-cap` if you need to approach a target). With **matched seeds** (default), tetanus and death “averted” counts usually move in the expected direction; if you use **different** `--seed-intervention`, comparisons can look noisy. Prefer larger `--n-agents` if any metric is unstable.

Illustrative **global or national** totals in the source brief are **not** generated or validated here—only cohort simulation outputs.

Other JSON fields: **`relative_reduction_percent_model`**, **`projection_benefit_summary`**, **`projection_death_benefit_summary`** (pentavalent disease deaths aggregated, not tetanus-only).

## What this project demonstrates

Using an agent-based model (Starsim), we combine **administrative immunization data** (`zerodose_data_formated.xlsx`) with two modeled arms: a **reference scenario** whose delivery intensity is **calibrated** to the data’s mean zero-dose proxy, and an **intervention scenario** with stronger delivery. We report the **share of zero-dose children under five** (no modeled pentavalent dose), aligned with language for children who have not received an initial DTP-containing dose (see WHO).

**Pediatric-cohort focus:** The simulation initialises the population with **children aged 0–5 years only** (uniform distribution across single-year age groups 0–4). `ss.Births` continuously adds new agents at age 0 and `ss.Deaths` applies background mortality, so the under-5 sub-population is always replenished. The vaccination intervention targets **ages 0–60 months** (`age_min=0`, `age_max=60`). All burden metrics are therefore dominated by the childhood segments rather than the adult population.

By default the model **projects forward about thirty calendar years** (e.g. **2025–2055** when the spreadsheet ends in 2024): yearly zero-dose trajectories for baseline vs intervention, benefit summaries, **disease-attributable deaths** from the pentavalent disease modules, and figures including `projection_zerodose_20y.png` and `projection_disease_deaths.png`.

**Trusted context (not generated by this code):** WHO publishes global zero-dose counts and DTP coverage annually, for example:

- [Immunization coverage — WHO fact sheet](https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/immunization-coverage)  
  (2024: **14.3 million** zero-dose children globally; DTP3 coverage **85%**. Source: WHO/UNICEF, July 2025.)

**Kenya-specific official estimates** (verified April 2026 against primary sources):

| Source | DTP1 | DTP3 | Zero-dose implied |
|--------|------|------|-------------------|
| WUENIC 2023 revision (survey-adjusted) | 97% | 93% | ~3% |
| WUENIC 2024 revision (survey-adjusted) | 99% | 95% | ~1% |
| Administrative coverage 2023 (WUENIC country notes) | 83% | 85% | ~17% |
| KDHS 2022 (card/history, recall-bias-adjusted) | ~97% | ~93% | ~3–7% |

The model's calibration zero-dose proxy (~16.5%) is derived from **raw administrative counts** in `zerodose_data_formated.xlsx`, consistent with the administrative column above. WUENIC's headline estimates are calibrated upward using KDHS survey evidence. Both are valid for their purpose.

Kenya has achieved **Maternal and Neonatal Tetanus Elimination** (WHO/CDC); the tetanus module here models general wound-exposure tetanus burden, not a high-burden endemic scenario.

For **country- or region-specific** coverage, use official WHO/UNICEF tools (e.g. WUENIC / Global Health Observatory), not numbers invented here.

### Policy alignment (zero-dose and Immunization Agenda 2030)

Global and national reporting describes **zero-dose** children as those who have not received even a first dose of DTP-containing vaccine (often tracked alongside pentavalent programs). **Equity** and **last-mile** access—rural and arid regions, informal settlements, conflict-affected areas—are central to why coverage gaps persist; **data quality** and **political commitment** matter as much as supply.

**What this repo is for:** a transparent, **model-based** way to ask how much **incremental reach** from stronger routine delivery could reduce modeled zero-dose *share* and related burden in a cohort, when the reference scenario is **calibrated to administrative proxies** in your spreadsheet. **What it is not:** a replacement for KDHS/WUENIC headline estimates, county-level microsimulation, or validated forecasts for a specific Kenyan county unless you extend inputs and validation yourself.

## Mapping the Rono et al. (2024) brief to zdsim

*Methods correspondence note for the repository. The same article appears in the web viewer (`web/index.html`). Update Readme and `index.html` together; `docs/zero_dose_project_alignment.md` points here only.*

**Source document.** Betsy Rono, Mambo Susan, Anne Njeri, Raphael Akangbe. Project **2-2A-7**, April 2024. PDF: `2-2A-7_Rono_Zero-dose-vaccination.pdf` (keep your copy locally; optional path `docs/references/`).

### Abstract

Project 2-2A-7 outlines zero-dose vaccination concepts, Starsim-style modeling, and policy targets for Kenya. This note records how that brief aligns with **zdsim** and `run_simulation.py`: which definitions and parameters match, which differ, and which claims require external survey or WHO sources rather than simulation output. It supports reviewers and reproducibility; it does not replace the original PDF.

**Keywords:** zero-dose children; DTP; pentavalent vaccine; Starsim; agent-based model; Kenya; Immunization Agenda 2030

### 1. Introduction

The brief situates zero-dose burden in KEPI/Gavi and WHO terms, poses the **tetanus cases averted / 50% zero-dose by 2025** research question, and proposes quantitative illustrations (including tetanus-related parameters and intervention assumptions). What the code does and does **not** report for that question is summarized at the top of this file under **Research question**. **zdsim** implements an agent-based Starsim simulation with administrative calibration and two delivery arms (reference vs intervention). This note does not reproduce the brief’s slides; it states what the code implements so readers can compare statements in the PDF to executable choices in the repository.

### 2. Terminology and analytic scope

| Brief | Implementation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Zero-dose (Gavi / KEPI):** no first DTP-containing dose (DTP1), per NVIP MoH 2023 | Modeled as **no pentavalent dose** in `ZeroDoseVaccination`; administrative **DTP1 proxy** from `zerodose_data_formated.xlsx` drives calibration. |
| **IA2030** | Described in Readme and SPA mission text; not a separate module. |
| **Kenya, under-five focus** | Cohort is **under-fives** in the ABM; **national/county geography is not modeled** unless you add strata or spatial data. |
| **DTP1 as operational ZD indicator (WHO)** | **Mean DTP1 coverage proxy** from monthly admin data → `intervention_coverage`; zero-dose proxy = 1 − coverage (see `zerodose_data.py`). |

### 3. Correspondence of methods: brief vs this repository

| Brief | Implementation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Starsim** | **Yes** — `ss.Sim` in `run_simulation.py` (`build_sim_from_bundle`). |
| **SIS structure in slides** | This repo uses **Starsim `Infection` modules** (S–I–R-style with disease-specific states), not a single SIS compartment for all pathogens. |
| **Tetanus: γ = 3/12 yr, waning = 0.055** | **Yes** in `zdsim/diseases/tetanus.py` (`dur_inf` mean 3/12 yr, `waning=0.055`). |
| **Tetanus β = 1.3** | **Different:** tetanus uses an **environmental wound-exposure model** with calibrated age-specific wound rates (see `zdsim/diseases/tetanus.py`). No person-to-person β is applied. The brief’s β = 1.3 is a teaching-model abstraction. |
| **Intervention: vaccination prob = 0.25, efficacy = 0.9** | **Efficacy 0.9** matches **`intervention_efficacy=0.9`** in the bundle. “Prob 0.25” maps to **`routine_prob × coverage`** in `ZeroDoseVaccination`; the **intervention** arm uses **scale-up** (`routine_prob` capped, higher coverage cap) — tune with `--scale-routine-factor` / `--scale-coverage-cap`. |
| **50% reduction in ZD by 2025** | **Policy target**, not hard-coded. Use **`--rono-2025-window`** (2024–2025 calendar window) for the brief’s horizon, and compare **reference vs intervention** relative reduction in modeled zero-dose share (`relative_reduction_percent_model`). |
| **Research question: tetanus cases averted** | **`research_question_tetanus`** in summary JSON: tetanus **cases** = new infections from the tetanus module; see **Research question** at top of this file. |

### 4. Software configuration for the brief’s policy horizon

```bash
python run_simulation.py --rono-2025-window
```

This sets **calendar 2024–2025** (overrides default `--start` / `--stop`). For longer policy projections, omit the flag and use e.g. `--start 2025 --stop 2055`.

### 5. External statistics cited in the brief

KDHS (~7% ZD), global WHO figures, county names, and similar quantities appear as **citations in the PDF**, not as outputs of this code. For publications, validate figures against **KDHS**, **WUENIC**, and **WHO** primary sources. Current official values are listed in the **Trusted context** section above; see also [WHO immunization coverage fact sheet](https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/immunization-coverage) and [WUENIC](https://www.who.int/teams/immunization-vaccines-and-biologicals/immunization-analysis-and-insights/global-monitoring/immunization-coverage/who-unicef-estimates-of-national-immunization-coverage).

### 6. Discussion

The simulation yields **modeled** zero-dose shares and disease-attributable deaths in a cohort; it does not estimate national headcounts or subnational coverage unless extended. Parameters that match the brief (e.g. vaccine efficacy) should be read alongside structural differences (e.g. tetanus transmission assumptions). Use `--rono-2025-window` when comparing to the brief’s stated horizon; use longer windows for extended policy projections.

## Quick start for researchers

Use the helper script `research_workflow.py` for the easiest workflow.

```bash
# Full study run (2025-2055), auto-calibrates once then reuses calibration.json
python research_workflow.py

# Faster check run (2025-2030, 5k agents)
python research_workflow.py --quick

# Paper horizon run (2024-2025)
python research_workflow.py --paper

# Force fresh calibration, then run
python research_workflow.py --fresh-calibration
```

What it does automatically:
1. Creates `calibration.json` if missing (or regenerates it with `--fresh-calibration`).
2. Runs `run_simulation.py` using that calibration file.
3. Writes outputs to `outputs/` (or `--out <folder>`).

Advanced users can still run `calibrate.py` and `run_simulation.py` directly.

## Run the demonstration

From the repository root:

```bash
python run_simulation.py
```

By default this loads **`zdsim/data/zerodose_data_formated.xlsx`** (monthly administrative counts).  
Do **not** point at Excel lock files named `~$...xlsx` — those are temporary; use the real workbook.

Defaults: **`--start 2025`** and **`--stop 2055`** (about 30-year projection). Override with `--start` / `--stop` as needed.

**Calibration:** Right before each run, the script reloads parameters from the data (or `--no-data` fallbacks): mean DTP1 coverage proxy → intervention `coverage`; grid search → `routine_prob` to match the empirical zero-dose proxy; optional **`--population`** (with data) sets crude `birth_rate` from `estimated_lb`. Full bundles are printed to the console and saved under `calibration_*_bundle` in `zerodose_demo_summary.json`.

**Defaults:** **`--n-agents 20000`** for both the reference and intervention simulations (presentation-style cohort size, rounded up from the prior 15 000 default). **`--seed`** (default 42) controls the calibration short runs and both projection arms; **`--seed-intervention`** defaults to the **same** value as **`--seed`** so the two scenarios share randomness (**common random numbers**) and differences reflect delivery parameters, not two unrelated draws. Use a different **`--seed-intervention`** only if you want independent RNG between arms (noisier disease comparisons).

Other options: `--data`, `--no-data`, `--out` (see `python run_simulation.py --help`).

### Web viewer (SPA)

After a simulation run, **`web/data/summary.js`** is embedded with the same data as the JSON (so opening **`web/index.html`** via **file://** usually works without a server). To serve from the repo and open the browser:

```bash
python web/open_spa.py
```

Or start only the static server (port may shift if 8765 is busy):

```bash
python web/serve.py
```

Then visit **http://127.0.0.1:8765/web/index.html** (or the URL printed by `serve.py`).

The run also copies **`outputs/zerodose_demo_summary.json`** to **`web/data/summary.json`**. If embedded data is missing or stale, use **“Load JSON”** in the page and pick `zerodose_demo_summary.json`, or serve the repo so fetch can reach `/outputs/zerodose_demo_summary.json`.

### GitHub Pages (published SPA)

The **`web/`** directory is deployed as the site root by **GitHub Actions** (workflow `.github/workflows/deploy-pages.yml`).

1. In the GitHub repo: **Settings → Pages → Build and deployment**, set **Source** to **GitHub Actions** (not “Deploy from a branch”).
2. Push to **`main`** or **`master`**. The *Deploy SPA to GitHub Pages* workflow runs and publishes `web/`.
3. Open the site at **`https://<owner>.github.io/<repository>/`** (the workflow’s *github-pages* environment also shows the exact URL after deployment).

The live site cannot read your machine’s **`outputs/`** directory. Refresh published results by running `python run_simulation.py`, then committing **`web/data/summary.js`** (and optionally `summary.json`) so the next push updates the embedded data—or use **Load JSON** in the browser with an exported `zerodose_demo_summary.json`.

### Example results summary

The table below matches one **example** run (`python run_simulation.py`, seed **42** for both arms, **20 000** agents, projection **2025–2055**). Stochastic variation will change model percentages if you change `--seed` or `--n-agents`. **Always check `outputs/zerodose_demo_summary.json` after your run.**

| Metric | Value (example) | Source |
|--------|-----------------|--------|
| Administrative mean DTP1 coverage proxy | **83.5%** | Mean over **84** months in `zerodose_data_formated.xlsx` (years **2018–2024** in sheet) |
| Administrative mean zero-dose proxy (1 − coverage) | **16.5%** (± about **6.4%** s.d. across months) | Same spreadsheet |
| Model zero-dose (under-fives), end of window — reference scenario | **~14%** | Starsim, reference arm |
| Model zero-dose (under-fives), end of window — intervention scenario | **~7%** | Starsim, intervention arm |
| Relative reduction (reference → intervention at end of window) | **~52%** | Same run |
| Mean annual gap (zero-dose share, intervention vs reference) over projection | **~8–9** percentage points (varies by seed/`--n-agents`) | `projection_benefit_summary.mean_annual_reduction_zerodose_share_pp` in JSON |
| Cumulative gap index (percentage-point·years) over the projection | **order ~100s** (scales with horizon) | `cumulative_zerodose_share_reduction_pp_years` — useful for comparing scenario runs, not a head count |

Year-by-year projected fractions are in **`projection_yearly_reference`** and **`projection_yearly_scale_up`** in the JSON.

WHO’s global facts (e.g. total zero-dose children worldwide) are **not** recalculated here; see the [WHO immunization coverage fact sheet](https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/immunization-coverage).

## Scope and limitations

- **Simulation outputs are illustrative**, not empirical estimates for a real region unless you calibrate to data you supply.
- This repository does **not** ship proprietary survey or facility data; scripts that reference local `.dta` files expect you to add those files yourself.
- A large set of exploratory scripts lives under `scripts/`; the supported entry point for the core demo is `run_simulation.py`.
